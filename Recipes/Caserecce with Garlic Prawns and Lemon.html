Caserecce with Garlic Prawns and Lemon

Servings: 4 Source: Anaffairwithitaly.com

Ingredients

• 20 green prawns, peeled and cleaned

• 1 clove of garlic, minced

• 5 tablespoons of extra-virgin olive oil + some for drizzling on top

• 2 tablespoons of finely chopped parsley stalks

• 1 chillie, finely chopped (optional)

• 150 ml of white wine

• salt for seasoning

• 400 gr of Casarecce (or cavatelli)

• parsley and finely grated lemon zest lemon zest

Directions

1. Place the prawns, garlic and 2 tablespoons of oil in a bowl, massage them with your hands, cover with plastic film and leave in the fridge to marinate for a minimum of 2 hours, up to 24.

2. Bring a large pot of salted water to the boil

3. In the meantime, make the sauce. Heat up the remaining oil in a large, heavy-based pan, add the parsley stalks, the chillie and the prawns and cook over high heat for 1-2 minutes. Pour in the wine and cook out the alcohol for 1-2 minutes, then turn the heat off. Taste for salt and adjust to your liking.

4. Return to the pasta pot. When it comes to a forceful boil, drop the pasta in and stir with a wooden spoon. Cook for 8-9 minutes or according to packet instruction to a perfect al dente. Heat up the pan with the prawn sauce. Using a slotted spoon, lift the pasta out of the pot and drop it straight into the prawn pan, along with a little pasta cooking water. Toss the pasta in the sauce until well coated. Turn off the heat. Serve topped with torn parsley leaves, freshly grated lemon zest and a drizzle of extra-virgin olive oil.
