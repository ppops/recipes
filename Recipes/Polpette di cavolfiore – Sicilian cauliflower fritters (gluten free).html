Polpette di cavolfiore – Sicilian cauliflower fritters (gluten free)

Prep Time: 10 minutes Cook Time: 30 mins Servings: 20 fritters Source: Italyonmymind.com.au

Ingredients

1 cauliflower, trimmed and cut into florets (about 600g of florets)

1 small onion, finely diced (about 100g)

30 g pine nuts, toasted

30g currants, soaked in warm water for 10 minutes

salt and pepper, to taste

1 extra-large egg

100g parmigiano cheese, grated (approximate)

parsley, leaves picked and chopped

50g gluten free breadcrumbs (approximate)

Flour (gluten free) for rolling the fritters

olive oil (for cooking)

Directions

Boil some salted water in a large pot and add the cauliflower florets until fork tender (mine took just under 10 minutes). Drain. In a large saucepan, heat some olive oil and saute’ the onion for around 7 miutes, until softened and starting to caramelise. Increase the heat and add the cauliflower florets, the pine nuts and the drained currants. Cook for around 5 minutes, stirring frequently then reduce the heat and mash the cauliflower with a fork as you cook. Season with salt and pepper and cook for another 7 minutes approximately. Remove form the heat and allow to cool completely.

In a large bowl, place the cooled cauliflower mixture, the cheese, parsley leaves and egg. Add a tablespoon of breadcrumbs at a time, mixing as you go, until the mixture holds together. You should aim to add just enough breadcrumbs so that the fritters stay together when you cook them. Roll individual balls in flour and flatten them slightly.

Pan fry in medium-hot olive oil for a few minutes on each side until golden. Drain on absorbent paper and serve warm or at room temperature.
