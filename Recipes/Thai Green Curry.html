Thai Green Curry

Prep Time: 1 hr 30 mins Cook Time: 15 mins Difficulty: Medium Servings: 4 Source: Kata Thai Cooking Class

Ingredients

Green Curry Taste

12 fresh green chilli

5 fresh red chilli (the smaller the hotter)

3 tbsp lemongrass (chop the white end section of the sticks)

1 tbsp chopped galangal (looks like ginger)

1/4 tsp skin if kaffir lime

1 tsp coriander root

9 cloves garlic

5 cloves shallot

1 tsp roasted coriander seed

1/2 tsp roasted cumin seed

10-15 black pepper seeds

1 tsp shrimp paste

2-3 tsp salt

Water


Green Curry

1/2 -1 tbsp green curry paste (according to spice preference)

1 can coconut milk

1 tbsp vegetable oil

1/2 -1 tsp palm sugar (or sugar of you don't have palm sugar)

1/2 cup water (if needed)

6 Thai eggplants

1 handful pea eggplant

1/2 cup thai basil leaves

1/2 tbsp soy or fish sauce

Meat - chicken, prawns etc

Directions

Green Curry Paste

Chop finely the lemongrass, galangal, coriander root, garlic, shallot, and chilli. Leave one red chilli for garnish.

Start blending roasted coriander seed, cumin seed and black pepper (use mortar and pestle if needed). Leave aside.

Blend skin of kaffir lime, coriander root followed by galangal, lemongrass, garlic, shallot and lastly put chilli. Add the seed and pepper mix previously blended.

Add water to combine (or coconut milk if you are going to eat straight away). Add just enough to not cover the mix (just under).

Curry Mix

Add vegetable oil to a pan and add the curry paste. Stir until you smell the curry (around 20 seconds). Add 1/4 coconut milk can to the pan and dissolve the paste. Let boil for 1 minute. Another another 1/4 can of coconut milk and wait until boiled. Continue with the remaining coconut milk.

Add diced chicken or prawns and wait until they are cooked (don't stir until chicken is 80% cooked through).

Add water if curry is too think or more coconut milk to pan and wait until boiled evenly. Taste the curry and add vegetables. Stir occasionally until vegetables are cooked. Then add palm sugar, soy or fish sauce to the pan.

Garnish with red chilli and thai basil leaves.
