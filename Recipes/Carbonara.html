Carbonara

Source: Pastacarbonara.com.au

Ingredients

500g pasta

250g pancetta/pig's cheek or bacon, sliced

Dash of olive oil

Salt

Cracked black pepper

5 eggs

150g parmigiano or pecorino romano, grated

1 cup chopped parsley

Directions

Cook the pasta in a large pot of salted, boiling water. While the pasta cooks, break all the eggs into a bowl and whisk in well the grated cheese and chopped parsley. Place a large frying pan over the heat and add a dash of olive oil. Allow to heat and add the pancetta, cracked black pepper and a little salt, and fry until slightly golden. Remove the cooked pasta from the stove and drain. Add the pasta to the frying pan and toss together well with the pancetta so that the oil and pancetta cover the pasta. Add the whisked egg mixture to the frying pan and remove immediately from the heat. Continue to stir until the egg just starts to 'hold' and serve immediately. Serve with extra grated cheese and lots of cracked black pepper.

Notes

Spaghetti is traditionally used in this dish, but I prefer to use fettuccine. The key to making this dish successfully is to make sure the pan is hot when the pasta is added and still hot when the egg mixture is added to the pan. Pancetta or pig's cheek would be my preference to use in this dish, simply because I like the flavours of these two meats, but using bacon in this recipe will add more of a smoky flavour. Try the different meats to see which one you prefer. Pecorino can also be a bit strong in flavour for many, so parmigiano can be substituted or perhaps a blend of both cheeses.
